*After Cecelia F. Klein*

These creatures prayed within the corpse  
of their amorphous spider-god:  
recumbent, legs arrayed in spars  
(alluding to their ribcage), torqued  
apart—petitionary hearts  
to herd beneath a scarab husk;  
omnocular, eyes roseate,  
raised pedipalps in welcoming,  
invariable semaphore.  
Enshrined atop the parish-loom,  
inlaying suitors on the mesh  
of labyrinthine boulevards  
and underlying catacombs  
with iron nails in arms outstretched,  
she drank their deliquescing sins,  
which, sputtering past gargoyle throats,  
would ward her ageless maidenhead.
